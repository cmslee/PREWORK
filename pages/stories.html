Jujutsu Kaisen 2022 Happenings

Last August, a preview for the 20th manga volume of Jujutsu Kaisen was broadcast on screens across multiple buildings in Shibuya, one of the busiest areas of Tokyo and site of one of the story's major conflicts. Fans watched from the famous downtown crosswalk lit by the city's shop and street lights.

Something I love about this preview event is that it opens with the main character, Yuuji Itadori, calling down a "curtain," something curse-fighting sorcerors in the series does to keep the violence and carnage of exorcisms safe from non-sorceror eyes. Surrounded by screens depicting the ooze of the curtain falling, the illusion is one of being "let in" on the secret, hidden battles of the sorceror world. It is also a treat to hear Yuuji's voice actor (Junya Enoki) recite the curtain-calling incantation as that has yet to happen in the show.

As with many major manga series with an international fan base, the series has been particularly active with cross-platform marketing and collaboration. Notable examples from 2022 include a fashion collaboration with Dolce & Gabbana (pictured above) and a collection of Tamagotchi toys with Bandai featuring characters from the first season and, more recently, from the theatrical movie release, Jujutsu Kaisen 0.
